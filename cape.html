Only powers that are at least one of these types will be shown.

The categories of powers are taken from Worm, and the approximate breakdown is below. Any of the generated power text that's in blue is variable, and will be randomised each time that power is generated - you can use the retry this one button to reroll the current power to change those details.

Each time a power is rolled, the type may be any of the power filters that are enabled. If the power fits multiple categories, disabled ones will not stop the power from coming up - a striker/blaster can be picked if either striker or blaster is enabled, even if the other isn't, for instance. The filters you have enabled are actually saved to the URL of the page, so you can select your filters and then share the page URL to someone else to let them select a power from the categories you chose.

Roughly, the power classifications mean something like this:

Movers are what it sounds like - they move around quickly. Speed, flight, teleportation, that kind of thing.
Shakers affect areas, usually only the area around them, and it usually goes away when they leave.
Brutes are all physical; strength, durability, and regeneration are the sorts of things you'd expect from them.
Breakers are complicated, but they tend to create an effect that only applies to them, and in some way changes their state or their physical properties.
Masters control minions in some way, be they animated constructs, animals, or even other people.
Tinkers, as you might expect, build things. Advanced technology, beyond normal, doing things that might not even make sense. They usually have a specialisation that they work best in, or even that they can only work in.
Blasters create directed, targetted, ranged effects. Think laser eyes and you'll get the idea, but they're a lot more varied.
Thinkers have some kind of purely mental effect, such as precognition (seeing the future) or mind reading.
Strikers cause some kind of effect through direct physical contact. No contact, no effect.
Changers can change their forms, but usually it's more like a werewolf than a ghost - a different form, not just different properties on the same shape. They're close to breakers, but subtly different.
Trumps do something to or with powers. Maybe they strengthen (or weaken) the powers of others, maybe they can grant temporary powers, maybe they themselves get stronger depending on how many people with powers are around them.
Strangers mess with your perceptions; things like invisibility fall under this, but so does shapeshifting that lets them look like other (existing, real) people - even though that also falls under changer.
